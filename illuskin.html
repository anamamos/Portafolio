Overview

A skincare experience designed to build confidence.

IlluSkin is a direct-to-consumer ecommerce experience focused on helping users understand, trust and choose products that improve their skin over time.

The goal was a clear, approachable experience that communicates benefits effectively and supports confident decision-making.

The challenge

Clarity builds trust in skincare.

Users exploring skincare are often overwhelmed by options, unsure of what works and hesitant to commit.

The challenge was to simplify information, highlight benefits clearly and create an experience that feels trustworthy and easy to navigate.

Approach

Designed to feel clear. Built for confident decisions.

A user-centered approach to ecommerce UX, focused on clarity, readability and progressive understanding — letting people build knowledge as they browse.

The strategy drew on UX heuristics, ecommerce benchmarks and careful content structuring.

Design choices, guided by UX principles

Small decisions, compounding clarity.

01

Benefit-led product pages

Users understand what a product does from the start. Clear communication reduces hesitation.

02

Guided visual hierarchy

Attention moves through benefits, ingredients and usage — supporting natural scanning behaviour.

03

Simplified decision flow

Reduces cognitive load and keeps the experience intuitive, helping users move forward with confidence.

Visual system — colour

A palette built for softness and clarity.

The colour system creates a calm, approachable experience while keeping strong readability. Soft tones support the brand identity, and contrast is used carefully to highlight key information and guide interaction.

Reflection

Clarity and structure, doing the heavy lifting.

This project reinforced the importance of clarity and structure in skincare ecommerce. The experience supports understanding, reduces friction and helps users navigate information more easily. Future iterations would refine product education, improve benefit clarity and test different content structures to further support decision-making.
